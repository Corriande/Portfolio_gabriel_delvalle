Teaching Python Through Processing

A comprehensive cirriculum for those starting with no experience!

The core of the processing library is the ability to place shapes on a canvas. Approach this simple mechanism with the logical capabilities of python, and it can be expanded out to make dynamic animations, games, and interactive artworks.

I began developing this cirriculum when I began teaching Python to students ages 9 - 14 in September of 2023. Presently I teach 3 Python group classes a week and multiple private lessons.

The processing library is supported by Trinket.io, which is an ideal platform for beginner coders to store their code on an account they can access from any computer. Compared to other game oriented libraries like turtles or pygame, which abstract mechanisms such as collisions and animations, the simplicity of processing allows students to construct the logic behind every mechanism.

Through creative coding, math and logic are given a visual form, allowing the results of coding to be intuitively observed. Students enthusiastic about math often have eureka moments, understanding how math works in new ways by seeing its dynamics visualized. In my experience teaching these projects to a wide variety of students, the visual elements enchant and inspire students to learn, while the games are a source of addictive fun and pride.
